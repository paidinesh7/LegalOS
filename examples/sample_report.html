Plain English Guide

Key legal terms from this document explained in plain English.

Liquidation Preference (1.5x Participating)

What it means: Investors get 1.5x their money back first, then share remaining proceeds with founders.

Real-world example: Company sells for INR 200 Cr; investors get INR 60 Cr (1.5x their INR 40 Cr) before founders receive anything.

Why it matters: Founders may get zero even after successful exit if exit valuation is low relative to investment.

Protective Provisions (Veto Rights)

What it means: Investors can block major decisions like hiring C-suite, changing business, borrowing money, or paying dividends.

Real-world example: Founder wants to hire new CTO but investor director says no; founder cannot proceed without investor consent.

Why it matters: Founders lose day-to-day control of critical business decisions after accepting this funding.

Full Ratchet Anti-Dilution

What it means: If company raises next round at lower price, investor's conversion price drops to match, diluting founders more.

Real-world example: Series A at INR 4,800/share; Series B at INR 2,000/share; investor's price resets to INR 2,000, founders lose 58% value.

Why it matters: Down round punishes founders severely; founder equity shrinks even though founders didn't sell shares.

Founder Reverse Vesting (4-year cliff)

What it means: Founder must stay 4 years to keep all shares; leaving early forfeits unvested shares to company.

Real-world example: Founder leaves after 2.5 years; loses 62.5% of equity despite building the company pre-funding.

Why it matters: Founders become employees of their own company with forfeiture risk; creates golden handcuffs.

Deemed Liquidation (founder control threshold)

What it means: Sale, merger, or any deal where founders drop below 25% voting power triggers investor liquidation pref.

Real-world example: Strategic acquirer buys 30% stake, diluting founders to 24.5% voting; triggers 1.5x pref payoff despite no sale.

Why it matters: Founders cannot do minority transactions or secondary sales without triggering investor payout obligations.
