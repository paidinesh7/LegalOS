Shareholders' Agreement Explainer

A plain-English guide for Indian startup founders

What is a Shareholders' Agreement (SHA)?

The SHA is the binding, long-form agreement (40-80 pages) governing the relationship between founders, investors, and the company. Unlike the non-binding term sheet, every clause here is enforceable — small wording changes can dramatically shift the balance of power.

Where the SHA Fits

The SHA is negotiated after the term sheet and signed alongside the SSA at closing.

1

Non-binding agreement on key terms. Exclusivity period begins.

4

All documents signed, CPs satisfied, money wired, shares allotted.

Transfer Restrictions

ROFR (first right to buy), tag-along (minority joins a sale), drag-along (majority forces everyone to sell) — symmetry is everything.

What It Looks Like₹ ExampleFounder Impact

AggressiveAsymmetric — ROFR only on founder shares. Investor transfers freely to affiliatesFounders locked 3 years. Investor transfers without restriction. Drag by investor-class majorityInvestor has liquidity; founders are locked in
Unusual5-year founder lock-in. Investor drags at own-negotiated priceFounders can't sell 5 years. Investor triggers drag at any time after 4 years at own priceLocked in but can be forced to sell on investor's terms

Insist on symmetry — if you're locked in, investors should be too. Drag-along needs 75% of all shareholders and a fair minimum price.

Who pays when things go wrong — caps, time limits, and baskets determine whether indemnity is proportionate or a blank cheque.

What It Looks Like₹ ExampleFounder Impact
StandardCapped at 1x investment, 18-month survival, de minimis + aggregate basketsCap: 100% of investment. Survival: 18 months (36 for tax). De minimis: ₹5L. Basket: ₹25LFair protection with no nuisance claims
AggressiveUncapped "fundamental" warranties (broadly defined), joint-and-several liability"Fundamental" stretched to most warranties. Any one founder liable for full amount. No basketCap is meaningless. Any single founder can be bankrupted
UnusualUnlimited, no time limit, personal assets, consequential damagesNo cap. No survival limit. Personal assets available. Includes indirect/consequential damagesUnlimited personal liability — opposite of limited-liability protection

Cap at 1x investment, 18-24 month survival, both baskets. Never accept personal-asset exposure or consequential damages in an equity deal.
