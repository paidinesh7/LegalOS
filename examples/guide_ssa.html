Share Subscription Agreement Explainer

A plain-English guide for Indian startup founders

What is a Share Subscription Agreement (SSA)?

The SSA is the transactional document — the contract where the investor buys shares and the company issues them. While the SHA governs the ongoing relationship, the SSA handles the mechanics of the investment itself.

AspectSHASSA
PurposeOngoing shareholder relationshipInvestment transaction
DurationSurvives until new SHA or exitMostly spent after closing (reps survive)
Key contentBoard rights, reserved matters, transfers, exitInvestment mechanics, CPs, reps & warranties
Who signsAll shareholdersCompany, founders, new investor

Investment Mechanics

What securities are issued, at what price, and how conversion works — CCPS is the standard structure for Indian startups.

What It Looks Like₹ ExampleFounder Impact
StandardCCPS with 1:1 conversion, non-cumulative 0.01% dividend1L Series A CCPS at ₹1,000 (FV ₹10 + ₹990 premium). Convert 1:1 at IPO or 20 yearsClean structure. FEMA-compliant for foreign investors
AggressiveOCPS — investor chooses: convert to equity or redeem for cash + dividend1L OCPS at ₹1,000. May convert or redeem at cost + 8% cumulative dividendRedemption liability. May violate FEMA for foreign investors
UnusualVariable conversion ratio tied to revenue milestones1:1 if ARR ≥ ₹10 Cr; 1:2 if ARR < ₹5 Cr — effectively halves investor's pricePerformance ratchet. Miss targets → massive dilution

CCPS with fixed 1:1 conversion is the clean standard. Variable conversion or redemption creates cap-table uncertainty and FEMA risk.

Conditions Precedent

What must be done before the investor wires money — more detailed than term sheet CPs, with a critical long-stop date.

What It Looks Like₹ ExampleFounder Impact
StandardSpecific CP list with 45-day long-stop. Company and investor CPs listed separatelyBoard resolution, Section 62(1)(c) approval, valuation report, MGT-14. Long-stop: 45 daysConcrete, achievable, time-bound
AggressiveVague CPs + catch-all + no long-stop date"Diligence to investor's satisfaction" + "no MAC" + "such other conditions as Investor may require"Open-ended escape hatches. Deal stays in limbo
UnusualCPs requiring founders to fix pre-existing issues at personal cost"Resolve all tax proceedings. Regularize FEMA non-compliance. All costs on Promoters"Months of delay, lakhs in legal fees, deal may still fall through

Closed list of specific CPs, 30-45 day long-stop, mutual termination rights. No subjective CPs, no catch-alls, no personal-cost obligations.

Representations & Warranties

Factual statements about the company that, if untrue, trigger indemnification claims — every rep is a potential liability.

What It Looks Like₹ ExampleFounder Impact
StandardFactual reps + "to best of knowledge" qualifiers + disclosure schedule. 18-month survivalDuly incorporated, financials accurate, no undisclosed litigation (except Schedule X). Survive 18 monthsVerifiable facts with known exceptions carved out
AggressiveAbsolute reps without qualifiers. No disclosure schedule"Company has at all times complied with all laws." "No undisclosed liabilities of any kind"No startup can guarantee this — creates open-ended indemnity exposure
UnusualForward-looking reps + personal founder statements. Indefinite survival"Company will achieve ₹5 Cr ARR in 12 months." "Founders have no personal debts > ₹10L." Survive indefinitelyProjections as warranties. Personal intrusion. Claims years later

Insist on "to best of knowledge" qualifiers, a disclosure schedule, 18-24 month survival, and no forward-looking reps (projections belong in the business plan, not warranties).

Covenants

Forward-looking promises about what the company will or won't do — pre-closing (signing to funding) and ongoing.

What It Looks Like₹ ExampleFounder Impact
StandardOrdinary-course operations pre-closing. Maintain books and comply with laws ongoingPre-closing: operate normally, no new shares, no debt > ₹25L. Ongoing: maintain records, D&O insuranceBusiness continues as usual
AggressiveFreeze on hires, contracts, and capex during pre-closingNo hires > ₹10 LPA, no contracts > ₹5L, no capex > ₹2L. Ongoing: no pivot, no new marketsBusiness frozen if closing takes 60 days
UnusualPersonal covenants on founders — net worth, debt, travel restrictionsMaintain ₹50L personal net worth. No personal borrowings > ₹10L. 30-day notice before international travelPersonal lifestyle restrictions, not business governance

Pre-closing covenants should preserve normal business operations. Personal covenants on founders are a hard no — they're lifestyle restrictions disguised as governance.

Closing Mechanics

How the money actually reaches your bank — single tranche vs milestone-based, and the long-stop date.

What It Looks Like₹ ExampleFounder Impact
StandardSingle tranche, full amount at closing, shares allotted in 5 days₹10 Cr wired on CP satisfaction. CCPS allotted within 5 business days. PAS-3 filed in 15 daysClean. Once closed, it's done
Aggressive2-3 tranches tied to revenue milestones. Full governance rights from day 1Tranche 1: ₹5 Cr now. Tranche 2: ₹3 Cr if ARR ₹3 Cr. Tranche 3: ₹2 Cr if ARR ₹8 Cr. Rights based on full ₹10 CrGive 20% ownership but get only 50% of the money upfront
UnusualNo long-stop date. Investor-only termination rightInvestor can terminate anytime before closing. Company cannot. Combined with exclusivity still runningTrapped — can't close this deal and can't seek another

Single-tranche closing, clear long-stop date, mutual termination rights. If tranched, governance rights must be proportional to money received.
