# Scoring Rubric & Output Formats

Use these standards when assessing and presenting findings.

---

## Severity Levels

Every finding should be classified into one of these severity levels:

| Severity | Meaning | Color |
|----------|---------|-------|
| **Standard** | Market-standard clause, no action needed | Green |
| **Aggressive** | Investor-favorable beyond market norms, negotiate if possible | Orange |
| **Unusual** | Non-standard clause that deviates significantly, should be flagged and discussed | Red |
| **Missing** | Expected clause or protection is absent from the document | Red |

---

## Impact Scores

Rate the document on three dimensions, each on a 1-10 scale:

- **Control Score**: 1 = full founder control, 10 = investor dominance
  - Consider: board composition, reserved matters breadth, veto rights, quorum requirements
- **Economics Score**: 1 = founder-friendly economics, 10 = investor-heavy economics
  - Consider: liquidation preference type/multiple, anti-dilution type, ESOP pool placement, participation rights
- **Flexibility Score**: 1 = full operational flexibility, 10 = heavily restricted
  - Consider: non-compete scope, transfer restrictions, information obligations, use-of-proceeds limits, founder lock-in

Each score must include a rationale of under 15 words.

---

## Exit Waterfall Format

Present exit scenarios at three multiples (2x, 5x, 10x) using the actual investment amounts from the document:

| Scenario | Exit Valuation | Investor Gets | Founder Gets | Notes |
|----------|---------------|---------------|--------------|-------|
| 2x exit  | Amount | Amount | Amount | Key observation |
| 5x exit  | Amount | Amount | Amount | Key observation |
| 10x exit | Amount | Amount | Amount | Key observation |

---

## Negotiation Priorities Format

Present the top 3 negotiation items ranked by priority:

For each item:
- **Current language**: What the document says (under 15 words)
- **Suggested change**: What to push for (under 15 words)
- **Reasoning**: Why it matters (under 15 words)

---

## Plain English Explainer Format

For the 5 most important legal terms in the document:

- **Definition**: One sentence, plain English, under 20 words
- **Real-world example**: One sentence, Indian startup context, under 25 words
- **Why it matters**: One sentence, founder perspective, under 20 words

Pick ONLY from terms actually in the document. Prioritize terms with the most founder impact.

---

## Executive Summary Format

- **Overall risk**: Single word — low / medium / high / critical
- **Bottom line**: 2-3 sentences covering deal posture (investor-friendly, balanced, founder-friendly), the single biggest concern, and a clear recommendation
- **Must negotiate**: Exactly 3 imperative action items, each like "Push back on X — request Y"
